Fuel plan

The Mazda 2 1.3 carries roughly a 44 L tank and returns about 13–15 km/L on this mix of mountain and town roads — so a full tank comfortably covers each leg of the loop with margin. The plan below tops up at the three points with reliable, full-service stations, and treats Bo Kluea's small village kiosk as a backup only.

Good to know

Mountain road, take it slow

Route 1256 between Pua and Bo Kluea is narrow, winding, and often misty in November — budget extra time and avoid driving it after dark.

Carry cash

Bo Kluea and the smaller villages have limited card acceptance — withdraw baht in Nan city or Pua before heading into the mountains.

Walking Street is weekend-only

Nan's night market runs Friday–Sunday evenings only. Day 3 in Nan city falls on a Monday, so it won't be running — the riverside restaurant dinner above is the better bet.

Cool season packing

Late November is the start of Nan's cool, misty season — mornings on Doi Phu Kha and at the viewpoints can be genuinely cold, so bring a light jacket.
